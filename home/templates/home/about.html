About Me

Hello! My name is Chloe Sonsteng which I guess is evident by the name in the top left. Also, you probably already know my name or else you wouldn't have found this site. I am a 28 year old transgender woman from Portland, Oregon. I am also an aspiring developer.

I'm going to jump into a bit of my professional history, and then circle back to my development credentials. In 2014, I graduated with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Oregon State University. While in school, I applied, and was accepted into the MECOP program. This provided me the opportunity to work at two separate six month internships prior to graduation.

My first internshipwas at PCC Aerostructures from April 2012 to September of the same year. Here I worked more in a manufacturing engineering role, facilitating the manufacturing of industrial turbines. During this time, I also found the opportunity to teach myself Visual Basic for Applications (VBA).

My second internship took place in a similar timeframe, but one year later in 2013. This was at Daimler Trucks North America. At Daimler I worked in digital mockup to ensure that all design aspects of a truck properly interfaced, and that enough clearance existed between discrete parts to account for road vibrations, and translation of suspension. I was able to leverage my VBA knowledge, and further VBS to automate many of the clearance analyses necessary in this role.

Since graduation, I have been continuously employed at Altech Aerospace, although my role has changed several times. Altech primarily performs design structural analysis of aircraft repairs and modifications. When I began my stint at Altech, I was one of two employees. It was the owner/president, one structural engineer, and me. I was given the title of Design Engineer; however, due to limited design work much of my responsibilities were similar to those of a structural engineer. I also was heavily utilized for my VBA expertise. In the summer of 2015, I started balancing these responsibilities with those of a liaison engineer. Over the course of a year, I alternated between being in Phoenix and Portland every six months. While in Phoenix, I worked on site at a Maintenance, Repair, Overhaul facility to oversee freighter and water bomber conversions of aircract. These duties were to document deviations and issue appropriate repair instructions.

In the fall of 2016 Altech was temporarily acquired by Precision Aircraft Solutions. In the summer of 2018 the two companies parted ways. While a part of Precision, I administrated the Product Data Management system for their 757 frieghter conversion. I also worked as part of the design team to revise drawings as necessary. Upon separation, my roles resumed to those of a structural engineer and design engineer. I also adopted all IT responsibilities at Altech, in addition to continued VBA work.

In the summer of 2019, I pitched a web-based integrated stress analysis suite. At the time, all stress analysis tools were built in Excel using VBA which was not necessarily always the best solution. My pitch was accepted, and I began working on said suite. It is written primarily in Python using the Django frameworks with HTML/CSS/JS being used for the front end. This is a similar architecture to this site right here. The site is internal to Altech, and cannot be shared here; however, it has been recieved with nothing less than glowing reviews.
